WEEK 1: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

AI ki duniya mein aapka swagat hai! Definitions, types, spectrum, techniques, history, sectors — sab kuch cover.

🧠 Topic 1: What is Artificial Intelligence? FOUNDATION
📚
Word of the Day: "Paradigm" (पैरेडाइम)
Fundamental model of thinking. "AI represents a paradigm shift" = AI ne thinking ka tarika hi badal diya.

McCarthy's Definition: "The science and engineering of making intelligent machines, especially intelligent computer programs."

Russell & Norvig (1995): AI = "Study and design of rational agents." Agent = environment ko perceive karo + best action lo.

Simple Core: AI = Data + Algorithms milke machine ko train karte hain taaki woh khud seekh sake.

Socho ek student jisko tum baar baar examples dikhate ho "yeh cat hai, yeh dog hai" — baccha gradually samajh jata hai. AI bhi aise hi kaam karta hai! Data dekhta hai, patterns seekhta hai.

Google Maps traffic analyze karta hai → AI! Netflix shows recommend karta hai → AI! Gmail spam filter karta hai → AI!

Key Formula: Traditional Programming = Input + Code = Output | AI = Input + Output (data) → Code (algorithm) seekh leta hai khud!

📖 AI — Two Perspectives

(1) Academic Discipline: Computer science + Math + Cognitive Psychology + Neuroscience + Linguistics + Philosophy | (2) Practical Technology: Voice assistants, recommendation systems, autonomous vehicles, healthcare diagnostics

⭐ Key Points — Exam ke liye!
  • Term "AI" coined by John McCarthy in 1956 at Dartmouth Conference
  • Turing Test proposed by Alan Turing in 1950 in paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence"
  • Rational Agent = perceives environment through sensors + acts through actuators for best outcome
  • AI = Data + Algorithms | Core components: two — data and algorithms
  • AI systems learn & improve without being explicitly re-programmed — yahi difference hai traditional programming se
  • AI viewed as (a) Field of Study (b) Practical Technology in Products

🔢 Topic 2: Types of AI — Narrow, General, Superintelligent VERY FREQUENT

Narrow AI (aajkal ka AI): Sirf ek kaam ke liye. Siri sirf voice commands, Netflix sirf recommendations — baaki kuch nahi kar sakte!

General AI (abhi sirf theory): Human jaisi intelligence — chess bhi, cooking bhi, doctor bhi. AGI = Artificial General Intelligence. Abhi achieve nahi hua!

Superintelligent AI (future fantasy?): Human se bhi zyada smart! Nick Bostrom ne apni book mein iske baare mein socha hai. Abhi sirf concept hai.

🎯

Narrow / Weak AI

Task-specific, most real-world AI today, operates within defined constraints.

  • Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant
  • Netflix / Amazon recommendations
  • IBM Deep Blue (chess)
  • AlphaZero (games)
  • Face recognition systems
🌐

General AI (AGI)

Human-like reasoning across ALL domains. Still THEORETICAL — not achieved!

  • Reason in multiple contexts
  • Abstract thinking & creativity
  • Self-awareness potential
  • Active research area only
🚀

Superintelligent AI (ASI)

Surpasses ALL human intelligence. Purely HYPOTHETICAL concept.

  • Far exceeds human intellect
  • Self-improving systems
  • Nick Bostrom discusses this
  • Raises existential questions
⭐ Key Points — Exam ke liye!
  • Narrow AI = "Weak AI" bhi kehte hain | General AI = "Strong AI" bhi kehte hain
  • AGI current status = "Largely theoretical, NOT yet achieved"
  • IBM Deep Blue chess = Narrow AI ka classic example
  • Nick Bostrom = associated with Superintelligent AI concept
  • Narrow → General → Superintelligent = "evolutionary continuum"
  • Narrow AI dominates ALL current real-world applications

🎨 Topic 4: Specialized Domains — Generative AI & Conversational AI

Generative AI = Naya content BANATA hai. Blank page pe kuch create karta hai. ChatGPT text likhta hai, DALL-E images banata hai, Suno music create karta hai.

Conversational AI = Insaan se BAAT karta hai naturally. Alexa, Siri tumhari baat samajhte hain, context understand karte hain, jawab dete hain.

Fark: Generative = NEW content create | Conversational = natural dialogue enable karna

🎭

Generative AI

Creates new content from vast data patterns. Subset of ML.

  • Transformers: GPT-3, GPT-4 (text)
  • GANs: Images, videos, data
  • VAEs: Diverse outputs
  • Challenges: bias, authenticity, ethics
💬

Conversational AI

Natural human-like dialogue. Uses NLP + ML + NLU.

  • NLP: Understand language
  • ML: Learn from interactions
  • NLU: Extract intent & meaning
  • Alexa, Siri, Google Assistant, chatbots
🔮

Augmented Intelligence

Human + AI collaboration. Amplifies, does NOT replace humans.

  • Predictive analytics
  • Scenario modeling
  • Strategic planning support
  • Climate & financial tools
⭐ Key Points — Exam ke liye!
  • GAN = Generative Adversarial Network | VAE = Variational Autoencoder
  • Conversational AI employs 3 things: NLP + ML + NLU
  • Conversational AI = "synthetic brain power that makes machines capable of understanding, processing and responding to human language"
  • Generative AI generates: text, images, music, video, code, and more — NOT only text!
  • Generative AI challenges: authenticity, bias, ethical use, need for governance
  • Transformers architecture = powers modern LLMs including ChatGPT

📖 ML — 3 Core Types (MEMORIZE!)

Supervised Learning = Labelled data se seekhna (teacher bata deta hai sahi/galat). Example: spam filter.
Unsupervised Learning = Unlabelled data mein patterns dhundna. Example: customer segmentation.
Reinforcement Learning = Trial & error + rewards/punishments. Example: game-playing AI, self-driving cars.

⭐ Key Points — Exam ke liye!

  • Expert Systems = Knowledge Base + Inference Engine
  • CNN = Convolutional Neural Networks (images) | RNN = Recurrent Neural Networks (sequences)
  • DL uses ANNs with MULTIPLE layers — "deep" = multiple hidden layers
  • Computer Vision applications: facial recognition, mocap, fingerprint biometrics, self-driving, surveillance

📅 Topic 6: History & Evolution of AI DATES IMPORTANT

1950 — Turing Test: Alan Turing ne "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" paper mein machine intelligence ka sapna dikha ya.

1956 — Dartmouth Conference: John McCarthy ne officially "AI" naam diya. AI ka formal janam!

1970s — First AI Winter: NLP ne "commonsense knowledge problem" mein fail kiya. Funding cut. "AI Winter" = sardi aa gayi field mein!

Late 1980s — Second AI Winter: Expert Systems brittle (rigid) aur expensive nikle. LISP machines ka market collapse hua.

2000s — AI Revival: Big Data + GPUs + Deep Learning — teenon ka convergence! AI phir zinda hua!

2022 — AI Boom: ChatGPT release hua! AI ne mainstream society mein dhoom macha di!

🔒 Topic 1: Data Protection — DPDP Act, Data Principal, Data Fiduciary LEGAL TERMS
📚
Word of the Day: "Fiduciary" (फिड्यूशरी)
A person/entity entrusted to act in another's best interest. "Data Fiduciary" = koi bhi institution jo aapka data handle kare responsibly. Like a bank trustee.

DPDP Act = India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023. Yeh law protect karta hai India mein log ke personal digital data ko.

Data Principal = Jiska data collect ho raha hai — WOH khud! Student ya staff ek "Data Principal" hain — data ka owner! Passive "subject" nahi!

Data Fiduciary = Jo decide karta hai data kyu aur kaise use hoga. Colleges/institutes jo student data process karte hain = Data Fiduciaries. Inki zimmedari hai data safely handle karna.

📖 Key Legal Terms — DPDP Act

Data Principal: Individual whose personal data is collected. They OWN their data and have rights over it — not just a passive "subject." In academic institutes = students and staff.
Data Fiduciary: Any entity that decides "purpose" and "means" of processing personal data. Must manage data with consent, security, and transparency for Data Principal. Institutions = Data Fiduciaries.
DPDP Act 2023: India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act. Governs processing of digital personal data. Grants individuals rights like correction, erasure, grievance redressal.
DPDP Rules: Notified in November 2025 — operationalise the Act. Sets clearer expectations for recordkeeping, grievance handling, institution-level practices.
